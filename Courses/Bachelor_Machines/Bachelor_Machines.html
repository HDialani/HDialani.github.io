EE1E1 course lab onsite version: electric drives

Introduction

In this experiment you are going to experience the behaviour of real-life induction and DC machines and measure (a part of) the torque-speed characteristic of the induction machine. We use the term induction machine in this manual. In the circuit diagrams, it may also be called an asynchronous machine. They are the same machine.

Before you are allowed to do the lab work, you have to study the experiment procedures and the theory behind the experiments. After doing the lab work, you have to analyze the measured results, calculate and plot the theoretical torque-speed characteristic of the induction machine, then compare the measured results with the theoretical results. A report has to be be written and submitted to complete the lab work.

The deliverable of this module will be a course lab report as per requirements given in the manual.

Introduction to the setup

Please read

  • Chapter 14, section 14.1, 14.2, 14.3 and 14.5 of the book Electric Power Principles: sources, conversion, distribution and use.
  • Theoretical background section of this manual.

In this experiment, a DC machine is coupled to an induction machine and a synchronous machine on the same shaft. The synchronous machine is not used during this practical, as it is only included in the master version of the course. The machine speed is measured using a DC tachometer

The per-phase equivalent circuit parameters of the induction machine is shown in Table 1.

During the Lab Session

You can follow these guidelines to complete the measurements:

    Before the Introduction Explanation

  1. Take a photo of the nameplate of the induction machine and write down the nameplate data of it (Brand, Type, Nominal stator phase voltage, etc.).
  2. We want to connect the induction machine to the grid. Using the nameplate data, determine whether the induction machine should be connected in Y or Δ.

    3. Wiring and operation only the induction machine

  3. Using either a Y or Δ configuration, draw and connect the asynchronous circuit with the required wiring.
  4. Connect the induction machine to the grid using the DOL (direct-on-line) switch.
  5. Connect the Chauvin Arnoux F607 power meter to measure voltage, current, and power. Refer to the laminated wiring diagram and pay attention to the current direction arrow.
  6. Have the wiring checked.
  7. Start the induction machine and record measurements using the power meter. What do you observe about real and reactive power?
  8. Turn off the induction machine and the power to the table.

    9. Wiring and operation of the DC machine

  9. Connect the DC machine to the Siemens DC converter. Connect the rotor connections A1 and A2 and the field connections E1 and E2 of the DC machine to the A1 and A2 and E1 and E2 connections of the drive. Connect the T+ and T− connections of the tacho to the T+ and T− connections of the Siemens DC converter.
  10. Place a DC ammeter in series between the rotor armature and DC driver (A1) circuit and a DC voltmeter in parallel across the armature winding A1 and A2.
  11. Use the DC tachometer mounted on the shaft to measure the shaft speed. Connect a DC voltmeter in parallel to the tachometer output. The voltmeter reading tells you how fast the shaft is rotating, and the polarity of the voltage tells you the direction of rotation.
  12. Draw the schematic of the setup with all the instruments and wires. Show your schematic and setup to the TA consultant before turning on the machines.
  13. Start the DC machine and adjust it to reach 1500 rpm (use the tachometer ratio as a reference).
  14. Turn off the power to the setup.

    15. Spinning both machines at synchronous speed

  15. Spin the DC machine and try to achieve 1500 rpm using the tacho, as a hint look at the tacho ration at your table.
  16. Use the stroboscope to achieve 1500 rpm. Once you achieve this you can turn on the induction machine.

    17. Use the DC machine to drive the induction machine

  18. Fill in the values in Table 2. The values of cos φ and torque T can be calculated at home.
  19. With both machines running, vary IDC from −5 A to 5 A in steps of 1 A, and record all values in Table 2.
  20. After finishing, return the DC machine to the 1500 rpm setpoint. Turn off the induction machine first, then the DC machine, and finally switch off the power supply.
